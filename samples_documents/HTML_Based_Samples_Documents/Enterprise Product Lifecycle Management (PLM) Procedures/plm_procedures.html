Enterprise Product Lifecycle Management (PLM) Procedures

Version 2.1 - Effective Date: March 15, 2012

Introduction

This document outlines the standardized procedures for managing the complete lifecycle of products within our enterprise environment, from initial concept through retirement. These procedures ensure compliance with industry standards and regulatory requirements while optimizing the product development process.

The Product Lifecycle Management (PLM) system integrates people, data, processes, and business systems to provide a product information backbone for the company and its extended enterprise.

Product Lifecycle Phases

1. Concept Phase

The initial phase where product ideas are generated, evaluated, and selected for further development.

Key Activities:

  • Market research and analysis
  • Idea generation and screening
  • Concept definition and validation
  • Initial business case development
  • Preliminary requirement specification

Required Documentation:

  • Concept Brief (Form PLM-101)
  • Initial Market Assessment (Form PLM-102)
  • Preliminary Business Case (Form PLM-103)

2. Design Phase

The phase where detailed product specifications are developed and the product design is created.

Key Activities:

  • Detailed requirements definition
  • Product architecture development
  • Detailed design specification
  • Design reviews and validation
  • Prototype development

Required Documentation:

  • Detailed Product Requirements (Form PLM-201)
  • Design Specification Documents (Form PLM-202)
  • Design Review Minutes (Form PLM-203)
  • Prototype Test Reports (Form PLM-204)

3. Development Phase

The phase where the product design is translated into a manufacturable product.

Key Activities:

  • Engineering development and testing
  • Manufacturing process development
  • Quality assurance planning
  • Supplier integration
  • Pre-production validation

Required Documentation:

  • Engineering Test Reports (Form PLM-301)
  • Manufacturing Process Documents (Form PLM-302)
  • Quality Control Plans (Form PLM-303)
  • Supplier Agreements (Form PLM-304)

4. Production Phase

The phase where the product is manufactured, distributed, and sold.

Key Activities:

  • Full-scale production
  • Supply chain management
  • Quality control implementation
  • Product marketing and sales
  • Customer support establishment

Required Documentation:

  • Production Release Authorization (Form PLM-401)
  • Quality Control Reports (Form PLM-402)
  • Marketing Launch Plan (Form PLM-403)
  • Customer Support Documentation (Form PLM-404)

5. Service Phase

The phase where the product is maintained, supported, and enhanced.

Key Activities:

  • Product support and maintenance
  • Customer feedback collection
  • Product improvements and updates
  • Service operations management
  • Performance monitoring

Required Documentation:

  • Service Level Agreements (Form PLM-501)
  • Customer Feedback Reports (Form PLM-502)
  • Product Improvement Requests (Form PLM-503)
  • Service Performance Reports (Form PLM-504)

6. Retirement Phase

The final phase where the product is withdrawn from the market and properly disposed of.

Key Activities:

  • End-of-life planning
  • Replacement product transition
  • Customer migration support
  • Material reclamation and recycling
  • Product discontinuation

Required Documentation:

  • Product Retirement Plan (Form PLM-601)
  • Customer Transition Guide (Form PLM-602)
  • Environmental Compliance Report (Form PLM-603)
  • Product Discontinuation Notice (Form PLM-604)

Stage-Gate Approval Process

Each transition between lifecycle phases requires a formal review and approval process:

  1. Gate 1: Concept to Design - Project Initiation Review
  2. Gate 2: Design to Development - Design Approval Review
  3. Gate 3: Development to Production - Production Readiness Review
  4. Gate 4: Production to Service - Market Release Review
  5. Gate 5: Service to Retirement - End-of-Life Review

Each gate review requires completion of the appropriate Gate Review Checklist and approval from the designated Product Governance Board.
